ALT / VIS

ALT / VIS is an ongoing archive of experimental audio visualizers built entirely with modern web technologies. Each piece in the collection explores a different mathematical relationship between sound and image — transforming raw audio signals into dynamic geometric structures, flowing systems, and emergent visual organisms.

At its core, the project investigates the hidden beauty inside signal processing. Music, when reduced to data, becomes waves, frequencies, and amplitudes. Through JavaScript and the HTML5 canvas, these invisible mathematical patterns are translated into motion, color, and form.

The visualizers range from traditional spectrums and oscilloscopes to more abstract generative systems inspired by physics simulations, fractal geometry, and chaotic feedback loops. Many are designed as experimental "visual instruments" where sound acts as the driving force behind evolving digital environments.

ALT / VIS is also an exploration of how creative coding can reveal aesthetic structures within mathematics. What begins as numbers and algorithms can evolve into something strangely organic — a reminder that computation and art are often closer than they appear.

Now I must admit, I was still really learning how to code (and still am) as I was building this collection, so I must admit there was a fair amount of AI assistance in the development of these visualizers. However, I see this as a natural extension of the creative process — using all available tools to bring an artistic vision to life. The code may have been co-authored with AI, but the underlying concepts, aesthetics, and curation are entirely my own.

This project continues to grow as new visualization techniques are explored and new systems emerge from experimentation.
